# W8 — Tips must MOVE the search (walker anchor fix) — TOP PRIORITY BUG

**Branch:** `w8-tip-attraction` · ~40 min · fixes the observed bug: teams
camp near the last-known point no matter how many tips arrive.

**Own:** `convex/simWalker.ts` ONLY. (It already receives `tips` — no
signature or caller changes. Do NOT touch sim.ts; W5 owns its one edit.)

## Why it happens (read first)

Every Monte Carlo walk starts at the LKP; tips only re-WEIGHT endpoints
that happen to land near them. If no walks reach the creek, boosting
walks-near-the-creek multiplies zero. Probability mass — and therefore
the planner, and therefore the teams — stays glued to the LKP.

## The fix: credible tips become walk ANCHORS (this is real SAR logic —
a verified sighting is an updated last-known point)

1. Build anchors each call:
   - LKP anchor: `{lat, lng, sinceMin: simClockMin, weight: 1.0}`.
   - For each tip with `credibility * weight > 0.2`: anchor at the tip's
     location, `sinceMin: simClockMin - observedAtSimMin` (the subject has
     only walked AWAY from a sighting for the time since it happened —
     fresher sighting ⇒ tighter, hotter lobe), `weight: 1.5 * credibility
     * tipWeight` (corroborated/judged-high tips dominate).
2. Distribute each hypothesis's `WALKS_PER_HYPOTHESIS` across anchors
   proportional to anchor weight (min 1 walk per surviving anchor).
3. A walk from an anchor uses `nSteps = clamp(floor(anchor.sinceMin /
   SIM.WALK_STEP_SIM_MIN), 4, 96)` — everything else (behaviors, terrain
   bias, deposit) unchanged.
4. KEEP the existing endpoint-proximity boost — anchors move the mass,
   the boost sharpens it.
5. Deterministic RNG stays (same seeded generator, anchor loop outside
   the walk loop).

## Accept (this is the user-visible bug, verify it visually)

Fresh case → run → let it settle 3 ticks. Type: "credible report, she was
seen at the north creek 30 minutes ago". Within 2–3 ticks: a distinct hot
lobe AT the creek (not just a brighter LKP blob), and the planner
re-tasks at least one team toward it. Then add the far-south red-herring
style tip — judge scores it low, and NO lobe forms there (anchor
threshold filters it). Both behaviors are pitch lines.
